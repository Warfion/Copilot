# Achievement Synthesizer
## Purpose
A comprehensive prompt that gathers evidence of your accomplishments across email, Teams,
calendar, and files — then synthesizes them into an executive-quality slide deck, achievement
document, and stakeholder email.

Best used with: Microsoft 365 Copilot Cowork

## Prompt
I need you to analyze my work and accomplishments for [TIME PERIOD — e.g., "Q1 2026"
/ "the last 90 days" / "this fiscal year"] and produce a complete, executive-quality
presentation package. Work through the following phases in order, completing each
before moving to the next.
---
### PHASE 1 — EVIDENCE GATHERING (run all searches in parallel)
Search across my email, Teams messages, calendar, and files for signals of completed
work, customer impact, and business outcomes. Cast a wide net. Look for:
- Customer names I've worked with and what we accomplished together
- Projects, workloads, or migrations that reached a milestone or went live
- Competitive wins or displacement events
- Azure consumption growth, ACR, or revenue-related outcomes
- Executive stakeholder engagements (EBCs, QBRs, exec briefings, exec sponsors)
- Technical blockers I resolved or escalated to resolution
- Internal contributions: collateral created, community posts, enablement sessions,
peer assists, deal support provided to other sellers
- Positive customer feedback, NPS mentions, compliments, or escalations resolved
- Any cross-team or cross-org influence moments
Do not filter yet. Capture everything with evidence (email subject, Teams message
date, file name, calendar event title) so each item is traceable.
---
### PHASE 2 — ANALYSIS AND SCORING
Now reason through what you found. For each item:
1. CLASSIFY it into one of these categories:
- Customer Outcome (something the customer achieved because of my work)
- Business Impact (revenue, consumption, ACR, pipeline influence)
- Technical Leadership (complex problem solved, architecture delivered,
blocker removed)
- Strategic Relationship (exec engagement, new relationship built,
trust expanded)
- Internal Contribution (IP created, peers enabled, community value)
2. ASSESS its strength on three dimensions:
- Specificity: Do I have a concrete outcome (numbers, names, dates)?
- Scale: How significant is the impact (customer-level vs. org-level)?
- Recency: Does it represent current momentum?
3. PRIORITIZE: Rank the top 8–12 items that tell the strongest, most
well-rounded story. Explicitly call out any gaps — categories with thin
evidence — so I know where the story is weaker.
4. BUILD A NARRATIVE SPINE: In 3–5 sentences, describe the through-line
story these wins tell. What was the theme of my work this period?
What kind of professional does this evidence show me to be?
Show me this analysis before generating any deliverables. Pause here and
confirm with me if you find the evidence is thin or the story feels incomplete
before proceeding.
---
### PHASE 3 — DELIVERABLE GENERATION (run all three in parallel once Phase 2 is
confirmed)
Using only the evidence from Phase 2, produce the following simultaneously:
DELIVERABLE A — SLIDE DECK
Create a PowerPoint presentation structured as:
Slide 1: Title — "[My Name] | [Time Period] Achievements & Impact"
Slide 2: Executive Summary — the narrative spine in 4 bullets
Slide 3–5: Top Customer Outcomes — one story per slide, with:
- Customer context (industry, challenge)
- What I did (specific actions)
- Outcome achieved (measurable where possible)
- Strategic significance (why it matters)
Slide 6: Business Impact — consumption, ACR, pipeline numbers in visual form
Slide 7: Technical Leadership Highlights — 3–5 bullets with specifics
Slide 8: Internal Contributions & Multiplier Effect
Slide 9: What's Next — 2–3 forward-looking priorities for next period
Design principles: Executive-ready. Data-forward. One key idea per slide.
No bullet walls — use visuals, callout boxes, and data highlights where possible.
DELIVERABLE B — WORD DOCUMENT
Create a detailed achievement summary document structured as:
- Executive Summary (1 paragraph)
- Full evidence log: every win from Phase 2 with category, evidence source,
and outcome description (2–4 sentences each)
- Metrics summary table: all quantitative outcomes in one view
- Narrative context: 1–2 paragraphs explaining the strategic environment
I was operating in and why the outcomes are meaningful
This document is the "source of truth" behind the deck — more detailed
and suitable for a written performance review or manager discussion.
DELIVERABLE C — STAKEHOLDER EMAIL
Draft an email to [AUDIENCE — e.g., "my manager [name]" / "my leadership team" /
"my extended team"] with:
- Subject line that conveys momentum and impact
- Opening: the narrative spine (2–3 sentences)
- 4–5 highlight bullets — one per category, most impactful first
- Forward-looking close: what I'm focused on next and what support I need
- Tone: confident, specific, not boastful — let the outcomes speak
---
### PHASE 4 — QUALITY CHECK
Before delivering, validate:
- Every claim in the deck and document traces to a specific piece of evidence
from Phase 1
- No fabricated statistics, names, or outcomes
- The deck tells a coherent story from slide 1 to slide 9 — not a list of
disconnected wins
- The email is tight enough to read in under 90 seconds
Flag anything where evidence was thin and you had to generalize. I'd rather
know the gap than have polished slides built on weak foundations.
